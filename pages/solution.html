Learn how to protect the environment and enjoy a cleaner, greener park!

Park Sustainability Tips

Saving Water

Use rainwater collection and eco-friendly watering systems to conserve water and protect park resources.

Protecting Wildlife

Keep ponds clean, avoid disturbing nests, and respect natural habitats.

Conserving Energy

Solar lamps and natural shade reduce energy use. Encourage energy-saving behaviors for a greener park.

Planting Native Plants

Native plants support local wildlife, need less water, and help maintain the park ecosystem naturally.

Reducing Waste

Recycle, compost, and use bins properly to reduce waste in the park. Small actions keep the park clean.

Quick Visitor Tips

Bring a Reusable Bottle

Reduce plastic waste by using a reusable water bottle.

Reusable bottles help reduce plastic waste.

  • Carry your own bottle.
  • Refill at water stations.
  • Encourage friends too.

Join Clean-Up Events

Participate in park clean-ups to help the environment.

Clean-up events help keep the park beautiful.

  • Join clean-up days.
  • Invite friends or family.
  • Collect and dispose of waste properly.

Avoid Plastic Bags

Carry reusable bags to reduce pollution.

Avoiding plastic can lead to a healthier planet.

  • Plastic bags are costly to recycle.
  • Most of them end up on landfill sites
  • They break down into tiny toxic particles

Use Recycling Bins Correctly

Separate trash properly for effective recycling.

Recycling and composting reduce waste sent to landfills.

  • Use recycling bins for plastics, paper, and metals.
  • Compost food scraps instead of throwing them away.
  • Encourage others to reduce waste too.

Walk Instead of Ride

Walking reduces emissions and pollution.

It will improve your health (fitness, mood, heart).

  • Walk instead of ride for short trip (1-5 miles).
  • Save money (fuel, parking).
  • Help the environment (less pollution).
