Why Parks Matter?

Parks cool cities, support plants and animals, reduce pollution, and create healthy spaces for people. Small actions—like using recycling bins, carrying reusable bottles, and joining clean-ups—help parks stay beautiful and healthy.

  • Cooler Cities: Trees provide shade and lower temperatures.
  • Cleaner Water: Plants and ponds filter rainwater and reduce flooding.
  • Wildlife Habitat: Native plants support birds and insects.

The importance of nature-friendly spaces

Improved air quality: Green spaces act as natural air purifiers, absorbing pollutants and releasing oxygen, which is crucial for public health.

Temperature regulation: Trees and plants provide shade and cooling, enhancing thermal comfort and reducing urban heat island effects.

Community interaction: Parks and gardens serve as social hubs where neighbors can gather, children can play, and fostering a sense of community.

Mental health benefits: Exposure to nature can reduce stress, anxiety, and depression, providing a much-needed mental reset.

Why sustainability matters for parks?

Sustainability is crucial for parks as it enhances environmental health, promotes community well-being, and supports economic resilience.

Ecosystem Services: Parks serve as vital green spaces that provide essential ecosystem services, such as air purification, water management, and biodiversity preservation. They act as "green lungs" in urban areas for city residents.

Stormwater Management: Sustainable parks help manage stormwater runoff by absorbing rainwater and filtering pollutants before they enter local waterways. (Learn How)

Biodiversity Support: Parks provide habitats for various plant and animal species, supporting biodiversity. (Learn How)

How Parks Purify Air?
  • Plant more trees and shrubs to absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen.
  • Use native plants to filter pollutants naturally.
  • Create green walls and urban forests to reduce air pollution in cities.
How Parks Manage Water?
  • Plant permeable surfaces and grass to absorb rainwater.
  • Construct rain gardens or bioswales to filter pollutants.
  • Maintain ponds and wetlands to store excess stormwater.
How Parks Support Biodiversity?
  • Plant native flowers and trees to provide food and shelter for wildlife.
  • Create ponds, meadows, or forested areas to attract diverse species.
  • Limit pesticide use to protect insects and other small animals.

Benefits of Sustainability in Parks

Sustainable parks help protect nature, improve community health, and build a greener future.

Protects Biodiversity

Healthy ecosystems support more plant and animal species.

Reduces Pollution

Trees absorb CO₂ and help keep the air and water cleaner.

Boosts Community Well-Being
